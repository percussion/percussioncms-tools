Handling Relationship Constraints

Relationship definitions may include constraints on the functions available for the Content Items. For example:

  • The Translation Relationship specifies that you may only create one Translation Copy of a Content Item for each Locale on your system.
  • When a Promotable Version reaches the public state, the original item moves from a public state to an archive state.

You system implementer may further customize the related content, Translation, and Copy Relationships in your system or may create new Relationship types with their own definitions. For example, your implementer could:

  • Change the default settings of Translations so that when you create a Translation Copy, only non-image related Content Items are also translated.
  • Change the default related content Relationship so that a Content Item cannot transition to a Public state until all of its related content is in the Public state or ready to move to the Public state.

Any number of custom rules may exist for the default and custom Relationships in your CMS. Familiarize yourself with the rules for each Relationship that you use. In general, if a Workflow Transition is not available because of a Relationship constraint, it will not appear on a Content Item's Action Menu.
