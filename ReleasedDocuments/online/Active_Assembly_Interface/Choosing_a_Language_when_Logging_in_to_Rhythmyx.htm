Choosing a Language when Logging in to Rhythmyx

Your Locale represents the language and dialect of the text on your Rhythmyx interface. If your Locale is French Canadian, the link names, tabs, menu options and other text on your interface is in French Canadian; if your Locale is American English, these components have American English labels.

The language of the text within a Content Item does not change depending on the Locale. To have a version of a Content Item in another language, you must create a Translation with a separate Content ID and translate the text manually.

After you log in to Rhythmyx, Rhythmyx displays a page with a Locale drop list if:

  • Locales are used in your system.
  • You have more than one Locale.

You can also access the page with the Locale drop list from the menu bar by choosing Account > User Info. The User Info dialog opens.

Click the Locale link to open a window that enables you to change the Locale. If you also have multiple Communities, the window allows you to change the Community.

The Select a Language drop list lists all of your Locales.   Choose a new Locale and click [Go]. You are returned to the previous Active Assembly page, but the labels should indicate that you are in a different Locale.
